> [!NOTE]
 > # Northern Reaches Campaign Summary
> ### Emberreach, The Tethynglas, and Veythar's Maw
> 
> The campaign began in [[Compendium/Atlas/Material Plane/Rushthan/Droshar/Northern Reaches/Emberreach/Emberreach\|Emberreach]], where a group of young hunters set out to investigate the death of Simon and a series of increasingly unusual attacks within the surrounding wilderness. What first appeared to be the work of a dangerous beast soon revealed evidence of deliberate biomantic alteration. The discovery of a Chimera site hidden within the Tethynglas raised the first major question of the campaign:
> 
> > Who was creating these creatures, and why?
> 
> Following the trail led the hunters deeper into the wilds of the Northern Reaches, uncovering signs of [[Compendium/NPC's/Tethynglas/White Fur\|White Fur]], an elusive biomancer whose experiments blurred the line between life, adaptation, and monstrosity. As they investigated, the party became increasingly exposed to unstable ley energy, resulting in mutations, strange abilities, and encounters that challenged their understanding of the natural world.
> 
> The search eventually brought them to [[Compendium/Atlas/Material Plane/Rushthan/Droshar/Northern Reaches/Crownkeep Mountains/Veythar's Maw/Veythar's Maw\|Veythar's Maw]], an ancient Annunaki site tied to death, passage, and the lingering influence of Titan energy. Within the Maw, the hunters discovered that death itself no longer functioned as expected. Creatures, hunters, and lost souls were being retained, altered, and recycled by warped biomantic forces concentrated around the Fountain of Pestilence.
> 
> During the expedition, [[Compendium/Party/Player Characters/Northern Reaches/Rho'Dan\|Rho'Dan]] was lost to the Maw and presumed dead. Refusing to abandon him, the party entered into an uneasy arrangement with [[Compendium/NPC's/Tethynglas/Seraphel\|Seraphel]] of the [[Compendium/Lore/Organizations/Amethyst Moon\|Amethyst Moon]], who sought the [[Compendium/Lore/Objects/Living Core Fragment\|Living Core Fragment]] hidden deep within the ruins. In exchange for assistance recovering both the Fragment and her missing envoy [[Compendium/NPC's/Tethynglas/Vael Tir\|Vael Tir]], she offered knowledge, guidance, and the possibility of restoring what had been lost.
> 
> The hunters ultimately recovered the Living Core Fragment, located Rho'Dan's remains, and witnessed firsthand the consequences of unchecked biomancy. Several members willingly embraced further mutation, believing adaptation was preferable to extinction. Others remained wary, seeing the same power as a threat to both humanity and the natural order.
> 
> Though Rho'Dan was eventually returned to life, the experience left him permanently changed. Choosing his daughter over the hunt, he retired from adventuring and left the life of a hunter behind.
> 
> With the Living Core Fragment returned to Seraphel, the party departed the Maw carrying more questions than answers. They had learned that biomancy itself was neither inherently good nor evil, but a force capable of both creation and catastrophe depending on who wielded it. They also learned that the strange creatures appearing throughout the Northern Reaches were not isolated incidents.
> 
> Their final hunt began when they discovered the destruction of [[Brakish Mire\|Brakish Mire]]'s homestead and the arrival of a new predator known as **Long-Step**. Unlike the Chimera before it, Long-Step represented a successful creation: intelligent, adaptive, and terrifyingly effective. Following its trail across the Tethynglas, the hunters slowly realized they were no longer tracking prey.
> 
> They were being hunted.
> 
> The confrontation ended within Long-Step's hunting grounds. The battle claimed the lives of [[Compendium/Party/Player Characters/Northern Reaches/Klein Feystin\|Klein Feystin]] and [[Compendium/Party/Player Characters/Northern Reaches/Chat Terfang\|Chat Terfang]], while others were left broken physically and emotionally. In the final moments of the fight, [[Compendium/Party/Player Characters/Northern Reaches/Jerek Valerian\|Jerek Valerian]] delivered the killing blow, firing a shot through the creature's open maw and ending the hunt at the cost of his own life.
> 
> When the dust settled, the party fractured.
> 
> - [[Compendium/Party/Player Characters/Northern Reaches/Rho'Dan\|Rho'Dan]] retired and returned home.
> - [[Dathan Blackwing\|Dathan Blackwing]] survived and returned to Emberreach carrying the story of the hunt.
> - [[Gummy\|Gummy]] departed south in search of the Mother Shroom.
> - [[Compendium/Party/Player Characters/Northern Reaches/Chaise Numen Jr\|Chaise Numen Jr]] vanished to pursue his own path.
> - [[Brakish Mire\|Brakish Mire]] survived despite wounds that should have killed him.
> - [[Compendium/Party/Player Characters/Northern Reaches/Klein Feystin\|Klein Feystin]], [[Compendium/Party/Player Characters/Northern Reaches/Chat Terfang\|Chat Terfang]], and [[Compendium/Party/Player Characters/Northern Reaches/Jerek Valerian\|Jerek Valerian]] never returned.
> 
> The Northern Reaches were left changed by the events of the campaign. The Living Core Fragment was no longer hidden. Biomancy had moved from rumor to reality. The influence of Titan energy continued to spread through the leylines beneath Droshar. And while Long-Step and the Chimera were dead, the greater mystery remained unresolved.
> 
> Someone had created them.
> 
> Someone had sent them.
> 
> And somewhere beyond the Northern Reaches, that story was only beginning.

# Session 24 — What Remains
Final State of the Hunters

Jerek Valerian
Status: Dead

Jerek's final act was exactly what a hunter's final act should be. Not glory. Not destiny. Not prophecy.
Just grit. Broken. Dying. One final shot. The pistol barked.The bullet passed through the beast's mouth and out the back of its skull.
The Apex died. The hunt ended.Jerek never saw what came after.

Klein
Status: Dead

Klein spent the entire campaign reaching beyond what should have been touched. Runes. Leylines. The Maw. Transit. Power.
He was perhaps the first to realize the world was stranger than anyone believed. In the end, he vanished into the violence of the hunt itself. 
No prophecy. No reward. Only another hunter claimed by the wild.

Chatter Fang
Status: Dead

The smallest hunter. The strangest hunter. Perhaps the bravest. His body does not return to Emberreach.
His story does not end with burial. His remains disappear into the care of another creation. Perhaps rebuilt. Perhaps remembered. Perhaps something else entirely.

Gummy
Status: Alive

The only member of the party who chose survival. When the hunt turned against them, he fled south. Toward swamps. Toward caves. Toward forgotten places where spores grow undisturbed.
Years later stories persist. Of strange mushrooms appearing where no mushrooms should. Of voices carried on damp air. Of something watching from beneath fallen logs.

Chaise Numen Jr.
Status: Unknown

The Chosen. The faithful. The hunter who walked furthest into the grey. When the beast finally died, something inside him broke as well. What comes next belongs to Chaise.
Whether redemption, damnation, revelation, or transformation. His path leaves the campaign unfinished. As it should.

Dathan 
Status: Alive

Dathan returned home.
The only hunter to walk back into Emberreach beneath his own power. He told the story. Not all of it. Nobody would have believed all of it.
He spoke of the hunt. Of Rho'Dan. Of Jerek. Of Klein. Of Long-Step. Of the friends who never came back.
He never spoke openly of biomancy again. Yet the changes remained. Years later, strange draconic traits begin to emerge. Power grows quietly. Patiently. Like something sleeping beneath the skin.

Rho'Dan
Status: Alive

Rho'Dan survived. Perhaps the greatest miracle of all. The hunter who crossed death itself returned to the one thing he valued more than any hunt. His daughter.
The leg never returned. Neither did the desire to chase monsters. For the first time in many years, he chose life instead.

Brakish Mire
Status: Alive

Against all reason. Against all probability. Against every sensible expectation. Brakish survived.
Again.
His homestead never truly recovers. Neither does he. Yet years later travelers still speak of a foul-tempered frogman living beyond the edge of civilization. Some things are simply too stubborn to die.
