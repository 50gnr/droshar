# Yūsha Village

> "Run fast enough and even thunder falls behind."

## Overview

Yūsha Village is a small coastal settlement situated along the northern shores of the Southern Principalities. Home to roughly three hundred residents, the village survives through fishing, whaling, coastal trade, and a deep respect for the sea.

Though modest in size, Yūsha is well known throughout the region for its maritime traditions and the legend from which it takes its name.

The village is named after the mythical hero **Yūsha**, a karasu tengu said to have insulted [[Compendium/Lore/Deities/Kaiju/Raiko\|Raiko]] before racing the Herald of Storms across the Realms and luring his destructive tempests away from the coast.

Whether the story is true remains a matter of debate.

The villagers insist it is.

---

## Geography

Yūsha sits upon a sheltered stretch of coastline overlooking the Sea of Songs.

Notable features include:

- Fishing docks
- Small whaling fleet
- Coastal markets
- Tide pools
- Sea shrines
- Rocky beaches

Most buildings are constructed from weathered timber and stone, designed to withstand strong winds and seasonal storms.

---

## The Pier of Yūsha

At the village's main dock stands a weatherworn statue of the legendary hero.

The sculpture depicts Yūsha mid-sprint with a lightning bolt pursuing him across the shoreline.

Children frequently dare one another to touch the statue's foot before running away.

Many claim doing so grants good luck.

Others claim it invites storms.

---

## Village Life

Life in Yūsha revolves around the sea.

Residents make their living through:

- Fishing
- Whaling
- Boat repair
- Trade
- Coastal harvesting

The community is close-knit and accustomed to helping one another through difficult seasons.

Visitors are generally welcomed provided they respect local customs.

---

## Yūsha Infirmary

The village infirmary serves as the primary medical center for the surrounding coastline.

It is currently overseen by [[Kalla Chiyū\|Kalla Chiyū]], a traveling healer of the Heirs of Kirin.

Kalla is widely respected by the villagers for her dedication and skill.

Despite her efforts, she is known to be deeply frustrated by several ailments that have recently appeared along the coast and seem resistant to conventional treatment.

---

## Horo Perono

Yūsha's most unusual resident is a ghost known as **Horo Perono**.

For generations Horo has haunted the village in a largely harmless fashion.

His favorite activities include:

- Eavesdropping
- Spreading gossip
- Startling visitors
- Complaining about everything

Most villagers regard him less as a spirit and more as an irritating neighbour.

Though he would strongly disagree with that assessment.

---

## Culture

The people of Yūsha value:

- Community
- Hospitality
- Perseverance
- Courage
- Respect for the sea

Stories and songs are an important part of village life, with many families preserving generations of maritime folklore.

---

## Economy

Primary exports include:

- Fish
- Shellfish
- Whale products
- Salt
- Driftwood crafts

The village is small but largely self-sufficient.

---


## Reputation

Throughout the Southern Principalities, Yūsha is known as:

- A humble fishing village
- A place of sailors and storytellers
- The village that claims to have outrun a storm
