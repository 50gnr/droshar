## Overview

Tucked away along one of In'nali's quieter canal districts, Sung & Gung is a favorite gathering place for laborers, travelers, shrine attendants, off-duty wardens, and the occasional Lotus Choir operative.

By day it serves tea, noodles, and conversation.

By night...

The sake begins flowing and the laughter grows considerably louder.

Few places in the city feel quite as welcoming.

---

## Description

Warm lantern light spills across polished cedar floors while paper screens open onto a narrow canal lined with flowering trees.

The aroma of grilled river fish, fresh tea, and cedar smoke fills the air.

Conversations drift easily from table to table.

Musicians occasionally perform in the corner.

Nobody seems to stay a stranger for long.

---

## Public Reputation

Locals know Sung & Gung for:

- generous portions
- excellent tea
- surprisingly good sake
- friendly atmosphere
- loud evenings

Visitors are often told:

> "If you can't find someone in In'nali...

...check Sung & Gung."

## The Basement

Behind an unremarkable storage door lies a surprisingly lively basement.

Here, patrons gather around a shallow circular ring where one of the establishment's oldest traditions takes place:

## Chicken Combat

Participants balance atop stout wooden barrels while attempting to knock one another into padded straw below using nothing but balance, footwork, and one shoulder.

No weapons.

No fists.

No injuries.

Only pride.

Friendly wagers are common.

Cheering is mandatory.
