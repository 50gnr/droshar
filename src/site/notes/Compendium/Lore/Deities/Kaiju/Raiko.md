# Raiko
## Overview

Raiko is one of the Five Great Kaiju of the Southern Principalities and is most commonly associated with thunder, storms, and righteous fury.

Unlike many kaiju, Raiko is remembered not only as a force of nature, but as a figure who has intervened directly in mortal history.

Throughout the South, tales describe him as a protector, destroyer, judge, and executioner depending on who tells the story.

Many shrines depict him as a massive tiger wreathed in lightning.

Others portray him as a towering humanoid warrior carrying storms in his hands.

No two accounts fully agree.

---

## The Iron Incursion

The most famous story involving Raiko comes from the ancient Iron Incursion.

According to tradition, invaders from beyond the world descended upon the ore-rich basin now known as [[Compendium/Atlas/Material Plane/Rushthan/Droshar/Southern Principalities/Tetsumine Basin/Tetsumine Basin\|Tetsumine Basin]].

For years the people fought a losing war against iron-scaled wyrms and armies devoted to absolute order.

Eventually Raiko was called upon.

Legends claim the Herald of Storms manifested physically and turned his wrath against the invaders.

Unable to remain in mortal form for long, Raiko instead shared a secret with the people:

the creation of thunderarrows.

Armed with weapons capable of shattering iron scales, the defenders drove back the invaders and secured their freedom.

To this day many communities credit their survival to Raiko's intervention.

---

## The Nature of Raiko

Raiko is often described as possessing a temperament unlike the other Great Kaiju.

While many seem driven by instincts beyond mortal understanding, Raiko appears capable of anger, judgment, and deliberate action.

Stories consistently portray him as reacting strongly to:

- tyranny
- oppression
- false authority
- broken oaths
- desecrated shrines

Whether these stories reflect truth or merely the hopes of those who worship him remains unknown.

---

## Storm Signs

Communities familiar with Raiko watch for several warning signs:

- lightning without rain
- distant thunder beneath clear skies
- storms gathering around mountain peaks
- static in the air
- unusual animal migrations

Such events are often interpreted as signs that the Herald of Storms is near.
