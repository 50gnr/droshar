## Overview

Bakuryō is one of the Five Great Kaiju of the [[Compendium/Atlas/Material Plane/Rushthan/Droshar/Southern Principalities/Southern Principalities\|Southern Principalities]]. 

For centuries she has been regarded as the guardian of the [[Compendium/Atlas/Material Plane/Rushthan/Droshar/Southern Principalities/Hakuryū Valley\|Hakuryū River]] and the countless communities that rely upon its waters.

Many describe Bakuryō as both river and ruler of the river, though whether she is the spirit of the water itself or simply its greatest inhabitant remains a matter of debate.

Regardless, few in the valley speak of her without respect.

---

## Description

Bakuryō appears as a vast serpentine dragon with flowing whiskers, river-polished scales, and a body capable of winding through entire waterways.

Witnesses often describe her movements as strangely graceful.

From a distance she resembles a living current more than a beast.

Some claim the river seems to move differently in her presence.

---

## Steward of the Valley

The people of the Hakuryū Valley credit Bakuryō with maintaining the balance of the region.

Stories tell of her:

- calling rains during droughts
- easing floods before they become disasters
- guiding fish migrations
- protecting wetlands and spawning grounds
- shaping the river's course

Whether these tales are literal or symbolic varies from village to village.

---

## Cycles and Change

Ancient stories speak of rare periods when Bakuryō behaves differently.

During these times:

- rivers become unpredictable
- floods worsen
- weather patterns shift
- old river routes reopen

Such events are often viewed as part of a larger natural cycle rather than acts of malice.

Most communities believe these periods eventually pass.

---

## Shrines of Bakuryō

Shrines dedicated to Bakuryō can be found throughout the valley.

Though each differs in design, most share common symbols:

- flowing water
- koi and river dragons
- scales
- spirals
- cyclical patterns

Offerings are traditionally made to ensure safe travel, healthy harvests, and favorable river conditions.

---

## Local Beliefs

Common titles include:

- The River Dragon
- Keeper of the Current
- Lady of the Hakuryū
- Mother of Tributaries

Farmers, fishers, ferrymen, and merchants all maintain traditions honoring Bakuryō.

Many settlements hold annual festivals celebrating the river's continued generosity.

---

## Signs of Bakuryō

People of the valley watch for:

- unusual fish migrations
- sudden changes in river depth
- silver scales found along riverbanks
- synchronized spawning events
- strange currents moving against the flow

Such signs are often interpreted as evidence that Bakuryō is nearby.
