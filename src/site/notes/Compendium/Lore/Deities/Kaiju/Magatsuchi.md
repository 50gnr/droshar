## Overview

Magatsuchi is one of the Five Great Kaiju of the [[Compendium/Atlas/Material Plane/Rushthan/Droshar/Southern Principalities/Southern Principalities\|Southern Principalities]].

Unlike the others, Magatsuchi is rarely associated with natural disasters or physical destruction.

Instead, stories describe a being connected to memory, forgotten history, and truths buried beneath the weight of time.

Across the South, elders warn that some things are forgotten for a reason.

Magatsuchi is said to remember them anyway.

---

## Description

Accounts vary widely.

Most describe a colossal creature carrying an immense golden-leafed tree upon its back.

Its body is often compared to stone, roots, and weathered earth.

Some witnesses claim entire shrines and ancient structures grow from its shell.

Others insist the creature is not a beast at all, but a moving piece of the forest itself.

No verified account agrees entirely with another.

---

## The Pondermoss Weald

Most stories place Magatsuchi within the depths of the [[Compendium/Atlas/Material Plane/Rushthan/Droshar/Southern Principalities/Pondermoss Weald\|Pondermoss Weald]].

The ancient forest is home to countless shrines, forgotten tombs, and overgrown ruins.

Travelers speak of paths that lead backward through history, voices carried on the wind, and memories surfacing without warning.

Many attribute such phenomena to the influence of Magatsuchi.

---

## Keeper of Forgotten Things

Magatsuchi is often linked to:

- forgotten rulers
- erased histories
- abandoned shrines
- unmarked graves
- broken dynasties
- lost names

Some believe the kaiju safeguards history.

Others believe it judges those who attempt to rewrite it.

No one truly knows.

---

## Behavior

Unlike many kaiju, Magatsuchi rarely displays aggression.

Its appearances are infrequent and often accompanied by unusual events rather than violence.

Witnesses report:

- forgotten memories returning
- long-lost paths reappearing
- ancient names surfacing in dreams
- hidden tombs becoming visible

Whether these events are caused by Magatsuchi or merely coincide with its presence remains uncertain.

---

## Local Beliefs

Throughout the Southern Principalities, Magatsuchi is viewed with a mixture of reverence and fear.

Common titles include:

- The Remembering Root
- Keeper of Forgotten Kings
- The Witness Beneath the Leaves
- The Forest Archivist

Some historians quietly pray to Magatsuchi before beginning important work.

Others refuse to speak its name at all.

---

## Signs of Magatsuchi

Stories describe several signs preceding an appearance:

- golden leaves appearing out of season
- forgotten names returning to memory
- roots emerging through old stonework
- vivid dreams of the distant past
- paths appearing where none existed before

Many regard such events as warnings.

Others see them as invitations.
