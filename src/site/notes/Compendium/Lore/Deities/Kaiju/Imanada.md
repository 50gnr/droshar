## Overview

Imanada is one of the Five Great Kaiju of the [[Compendium/Atlas/Material Plane/Rushthan/Droshar/Southern Principalities/Southern Principalities (41116)\|Southern Principalities (41116)]].

Known throughout coastal communities as the Lord of Sea Beasts, Imanada is associated with death, remembrance, and the passage of souls.

Many sailors believe every great creature of the sea eventually answers Imanada's call.

When the sea claims a life, it is said that Imanada remembers.

---

## Description

Witnesses describe Imanada as an immense whale-like being whose form shifts between flesh, spirit, and skeleton.

Its translucent body glows with pale blue-green light, while luminous organs drift visibly beneath its skin like distant lanterns beneath deep water.

When viewed from afar, many mistake it for a living island or a moving reef.

Its song can sometimes be heard long before it is seen.

---

## Lord of Sea Beasts

Legends claim that all great sea creatures eventually gather before Imanada.

Whales, leviathans, ancient predators, and creatures known only through sailors' tales are said to answer its call.

Many coastal traditions teach that proper burial rites allow these creatures to continue their journey.

When those rites are interrupted, misfortune often follows.

Whether this belief is superstition or truth remains uncertain.

---

## The Song of Passing

Throughout the South, stories tell of a haunting song carried upon the tides.

Some hear only distant whale calls.

Others describe feelings of grief, nostalgia, or longing.

Those who have lost loved ones at sea often report hearing the song most clearly.

Many cultures interpret this as a reminder that the dead must be allowed to rest.

---

## Behavior

Imanada is rarely hostile.

It does not appear to hunt settlements or pursue conquest.

Instead, it follows purposes that seem ancient and ritualistic.

When disturbances occur involving the dead, shipwrecks, or forgotten remains, sightings of Imanada often increase.

Scholars continue to debate whether the creature is responding to such events or causing them.

---

## Local Beliefs

Among coastal communities, Imanada is treated with deep respect.

Common titles include:

- Lord of Sea Beasts
- The Tide Shepherd
- The Mourning Whale
- Keeper of the Deep Song

Many fishing villages perform rites for dead marine creatures specifically to avoid drawing Imanada's attention.

---

## Signs of Imanada

Sailors report several signs preceding an appearance:

- unnatural fog banks
- ghostly lights beneath the water
- whale songs where no whales are present
- schools of fish swimming in unusual formations
- vivid dreams of drowned loved ones

Such signs are often viewed as warnings that something has disrupted the natural passage of the dead.
