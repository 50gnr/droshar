## Overview

The **Amethyst Moon** is a biomantic movement found in scattered cells across [[Compendium/Atlas/Material Plane/Rushthan/Droshar/Droshar\|Droshar]].

To its followers, the Moon is not a cult of destruction. It is a refuge for the changed, the rejected, the desperate, and those who believe the world is becoming something new.

To its enemies, the Amethyst Moon is a dangerous faction encouraging mutation, leyline instability, and voluntary surrender to forces no one truly understands.

Both views contain truth.

The Amethyst Moon believes transformation is not corruption by default.

It is possibility.

---

## Public Reputation

Most people know the Amethyst Moon through rumor.

Common claims include:

- They shelter the mutated.
- They encourage biomantic alteration.
- They interfere with Imperial stabilization efforts.
- They gather near Titan sites and unstable leylines.
- They believe the world is changing and that people must change with it.

In frontier regions, some see them as healers or protectors.

In Imperial territories, they are treated as extremists.

In places already touched by biomancy, opinion is more divided.

---

## Symbol

The symbol of the Amethyst Moon is a thin crescent moon split by a rising crystal facet.

---

## Leadership

The most widely known figure associated with the Amethyst Moon is [[Compendium/NPC's/Tethynglas/Seraphel\|Seraphel]], sometimes called **The Violet Midwife**.

She teaches that collapse and birth can look identical from the inside.

Not every Amethyst Moon cell answers directly to her, but her doctrine has shaped much of the movement.

---

## Known Activities

The Amethyst Moon has been linked to:

- biomantic sanctuaries
- Titan-site pilgrimages
- leyline rituals
- altered wildlife
- missing Imperial research teams
- refugee movements among the mutated
- strange violet markings near unstable regions

Not all claims are confirmed.

The Moon benefits from rumor as much as secrecy.

## Common Sayings

> “The world is not breaking. It is becoming.”

> “A changed body is not a failed body.”

> “The gods are gone. We remain.”

> “Do not curse the seed for splitting.”

> “What survives the next age will not look like the last.”

---

## Reputation by Region

### Frontier Settlements

Often viewed with suspicion, but sometimes tolerated when Imperial aid is absent.

### Imperial Holdings

Treated as dangerous biomantic extremists.

### Titan-Touched Regions

Seen by some as the only people willing to explain what is happening.

### Scholarly Circles

Debated heavily. Some consider them reckless mystics. Others quietly admit they understand leyline instability better than most formal institutions.

---

## Player-Facing Truth

The Amethyst Moon is not simply evil.

They offer real comfort to people who have been abandoned, altered, or hunted.

They also encourage forces that may destabilize entire regions.

Their danger lies in the fact that they may be partly right.
