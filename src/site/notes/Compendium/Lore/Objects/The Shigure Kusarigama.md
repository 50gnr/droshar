# The Shigure Kusarigama

### Appearance

The weapon appears deceptively plain.

The sickle is forged from a dark gray metal unlike local steel, its edge patterned with faint waves that resemble flowing water. The chain links are square rather than round, each individually engraved with tiny Dwarvish runes.

Near the base of the handle sits a clan mark:

A mountain reflected in still water.



---

### History

Nearly a century ago, a dwarven smith named Durn Blackcurrent left the western mountains and wandered the Southern Principalities.

Unlike most dwarves, Durn became fascinated by local craftsmanship and spirit traditions.

During his travels he forged only a handful of weapons, each intended not for soldiers, but for individuals whose work shaped history from the shadows.

One of those weapons was commissioned by an early operative of the Lotus Choir.

That operative was Nobu's grandfather.

The weapon has passed through three generations since.

---

### Reputation

Within certain circles, the weapon is known as:

The Chain of Settled Debts

Old Lotus Choir operatives recognize it.

A few retired officials recognize it.

Certain crime families recognize it.

None of them react comfortably.

The appearance of the weapon usually means:

Someone important has made a mistake.

---

### Why It Matters

The weapon itself is not famous.

Its bearers are.

For nearly seventy years, every known owner of the kusarigama served as a Cleaner.

Each left behind stories.

Not legends.

Warnings.

A governor who vanished after a failed coup.

A smuggler lord who simply disappeared.

A magistrate whose books balanced themselves overnight because every witness suddenly recanted.

The weapon became associated with the phrase:

"When the chain arrives, the matter is already decided."

# Hidden Data

# The Dwarven Runes

The runes along the chain don't contain magical commands.

They're a proverb.

Each link carries a single word.

Translated, they read:

"A debt ignored becomes a burden. A burden ignored becomes a disaster."

Nobu's father reportedly recited the phrase every time he accepted a contract.

---

# The Heirloom Aspect

What makes the weapon special isn't power.

It's expectation.

When Nobu carries it openly, older members of the Choir don't see Nobu.

They see:

- his father
    
- his grandfather
    
- every Cleaner who carried it before
    

Some approve.

Some worry.

Some wonder if he's worthy of it.

---

# Personality Tie-In

Strange Material

- Perhaps the chain isn't steel at all.
    

Maybe Durn forged it from a vein of dark metallic stone recovered from a mountain shrine after a meteor strike.

The metal never rusts.

Never dulls.

And carries an unusual blue-gray sheen under moonlight.

Then over 24 sessions, let [[Compendium/Party/Player Characters/Nobumasa Shigure\|Nobu]] discover that every previous bearer added something to its story. By the end of the campaign, the weapon's true value isn't that it's magical.

It's that carrying it means you've become the next name added to a lineage that the Southern Principalities remembers in whispers.
