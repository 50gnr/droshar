# Biomancy



> [!quote|no-t] # Biomancy
>
> *"The flesh remembers. The bone adapts. The ley reshapes. This is no art — it is the world’s will, and we only ride the current."*  
> —Siraen of the Ley

---

## Definition
**Biomancy** is the magical manipulation of living matter — flesh, bone, blood, and spirit — through the channeling of leyline energy. It is both an art and a danger, for the same forces that fuel healing, transformation, and adaptation can just as easily twist, corrupt, or destroy.  

In [[Compendium/Atlas/Material Plane/Rushthan/Droshar/Droshar\|Droshar]], biomancy is **not separate from nature** — it is nature accelerated, magnified, or redirected by the unstable power of the titans’ lingering essence.

---

## Origins
- **Titanic Legacy:** The first biomancers were the [[Compendium/Lore/Deities/Titans\|Titans]] themselves, whose very presence reshaped the land. Their remains still radiate energy that mutates and adapts life.  
- **[[Compendium/Lore/Objects/Leylines\|Leyline]] Surges:** Exposure to unstable leylines forced early creatures to change. Mortals learned to harness this process — deliberately invoking transformation.  
- **Giant Hymns:** Frost and sea giants once sang resonant hymns to harmonize with the leylines, shaping themselves without collapse. This practice is the spiritual root of biomancy.  

---

## Applications
- **Healing:** Accelerating natural recovery, closing wounds, regrowing lost limbs.  
- **Mutation:** Grafting monster traits onto mortals, or coaxing hidden potential from bloodlines.  
- **Companions:** Enhancing beasts to serve as guardians, hunters, or familiars.  
- **Weaponization:** Creating horrors of flesh, hybridized soldiers, or plague-beasts.  
- **Symbiosis:** Biomancers sometimes alter themselves, fusing with plant, beast, or stone to survive hostile environments.  

---

## Risks & Corruption
- **Instability:** Ley energy rarely acts cleanly. Mutations may spiral out of control, birthing monstrosities.  
- **Loss of Self:** The line between enhancement and transformation is thin — many biomancers lose their identity.  
- **Resonant Collapse:** Overuse of biomancy near a ley fracture can trigger **leyline storms** that warp entire regions.  
- **Ethical Strain:** In Emberreach and Steelholde, biomancy is both coveted and feared. Villages whisper of hunters “improved” by biomancers who no longer return fully human.  

---

## Cultural Views
- **Druids (Wildborn Circle):** Biomancy is natural adaptation, though dangerous. They see it as the world’s will expressing itself.  
- **Scholars:** Treat it as science — cataloging mutations, experimenting with grafts, and weaponizing discoveries.  
- **Empire:** Actively seeks to control biomancy as a military tool, outlawing its practice outside Imperial oversight.  
- **Common Folk:** Fear it as witchcraft. They tell stories of neighbors “changed” by ley storms, returning with eyes not their own.  

---

## Biomancy & Monsters
- Many of [[Compendium/Atlas/Material Plane/Rushthan/Droshar/Droshar\|Droshar]]’s most feared monsters are **biomantic accidents** — creatures twisted by ley surges or reckless experimentation.  
- *L'Arsenes Crafting Catalogue* (in-world) is sometimes used by hunters as a **biomantic field manual**, cataloging monster parts and grafting methods.  
- Hunters whisper that consuming the wrong part of a beast without proper rites may invite mutation — or worse.  

---

## Known Rumors
- **[[The Flesh Forge\|The Flesh Forge]]:** In [[Steelholde\|Steelholde]]’s undercity, an outlaw biomancer crafts hybrid soldiers for a hidden patron.  
- **The Stag’s Gift:** A hunter claims the [[Compendium/Lore/Creatures/White Stag\|White Stag]] granted them “perfect sight.” Their eyes now glow faintly — and they see too much.  
- **The Singing Bone:** A fragment of [[titan\|titan]] marrow begins to grow, reshaping anything it touches. A biomancer begs the PCs to help contain it.  
- **The Emperor’s Secret:** Rumors spread that the [[Emperor\|Emperor]] himself survives only through biomantic grafts, his body long past natural limits.
