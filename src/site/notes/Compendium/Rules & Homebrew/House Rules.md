# House Rules/Character Creation

2024 DnD 5e ruleset will be the primary source of guidance unless otherwise discussed.

## **Ability Scores.** 
We will use a standard array or pointbuy.

## **Encumbrance.** 
will be used in the way of a backpack slot system, outlined in your character sheets provided.

## **Multiclassing.** 
is allowed, but mixing spellcasting classes is discouraged. Powergaming and abusing masteries will be discouraged as well.

## **Relatively low magic campaign.** 
Casters are few and far between, there are laws that regulate magic use in public specific to certain cities/towns. Magic is feared/ frowned upon as it is a recent discovery and very uncontrollable in the eyes of the masses.

## **Long Rest.** 
8 hours on the road recovers half hit dice and removes one Exhaustion. In a Safehaven recovers all hit dice.

## **Short Rest.** 
1hr spent tending to wounds (using hit dice) and repairing gear. 

## **The Breather.** 
10-Minute rest) A creature can take a Breather to spend one Hit Die. When you do so, you don't regain any features that reset on a Short Rest.

## **Identify.**
A successful Arcana, Nature or Religion check (depending on the item; usually Arcana)-- or the Identify spell, is required to learn a magical item's properties and the way to use it during a Short Rest.

## **Attunement.**
Attunement to an item ends if the creature no longer satisfies the prerequisites for attunement, if the item has been more than 100 feet away for 24 hours, if the creature dies, or if another creature attunes to the item. 

## **Swimming.**
You need to be proficient in Athletics, Acrobatics, or Survival to know how to swim efficiently (at half your Speed); without one of these proficiencies, unless you have a Swim Speed, you can "float" and swim (dog paddle) 5 feet per round in calm water on a successful DC 10 Strength check, or DC 15 in agitated waters. If the Armor table shows "Disadvantage" in the Stealth column, the wearer has Disadvantage on ability checks made to swim, with the exception of padded armor.

## **D12 Random Encounters. **
Almost every time the players take a short or long rest or travel somewhere, you will roll for random encounters. On a short rest, one player rolls a d12 to determine if a random encounter happens; on a long rest, two players roll a d12 to determine if a random encounter happens. If any of the players dice match the d12 the DM rolled there will be an encounter.

  
## **Bloodied (Desperate Measures).**
When you fall below 50% HP for the first time you will be “bloodied”.

- When Bloodied you can chose to sacrifice 1-2 or 3 future ***death saves*** to gain powerful effects:

- 1: You can Reroll a failed attack, gain +5 on a failed check, or get a free Dash/Disengage.
    
- 2: Maximize Damage on a successful attack.
    
- 3: Gain an extra attack/magic action, or regain a spell slot of your choosing.
    
## **Dying Condition.**
When a character is reduced to 0 HP, they gain the Dying Condition. This condition is removed when the character is no longer at 0 HP.

A Dying creature suffers the following detriments:

Gains one level of Exhaustion at the end of combat.

Speed is reduced to 0.

On their turn the **DM** will roll a **Death Save*** behind screen.
  
When a Dying creature is dealt damage,  it is a automatic critical.

## **Death.**
When a creature is reduced to 0 HP, if the excess damage taken exceeds the character's Max Hit Points, that character immediately dies.
 
  
  

# Dungeon Dudes’ Fair & Fun House Rules for Critical Fumbles!

## Using these Rules

- If the d20 roll for an attack is a 1, the attack misses regardless of any modifiers or the target’s AC. Then roll to determine a critical fumble table below
    
- A character with the Extra Attack class feature can roll on this table twice, and may choose the desired result.
    

### CRITICAL FUMBLE TABLE

|   |   |
|---|---|
|d20|Result|
|1|Dangerously exposed. The target of your attack can use its reaction to either make a single melee attack against you with advantage, or move half its speed without provoking opportunity attacks.|
|2-3|Lost grip. Your currently equipped weapon flies from your hand in a random direction, landing 15 feet away from you.|
|4-5|Clumsy footwork. Your speed is reduced to zero until the start of your next turn. If you’ve already moved this turn, you fall prone.|
|6-7|Resounding deflection. You have disadvantage on your next attack roll.|
|8-9|Disrupted focus. You can’t take reactions until the start of your next turn.|
|10-11|Caught off-guard. The next attack roll made against you gains advantage.|
|12-14|Momentary opening. You cannot make opportunity attacks until the start of your next turn.|
|15-17|Poor aim. The attack misses with no additional effect.|
|18-19|Transferred momentum. The attack misses, but you gain advantage on your next attack roll.|
|20|Remarkable reversal. Instead of a miss, the attack hits instead.|
