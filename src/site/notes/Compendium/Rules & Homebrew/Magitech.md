# Magitech

Magitech is the practice of using magical energy through engineering rather than spellcasting.

While a wizard learns spells and a biomancer alters living things, a magitech engineer builds systems that produce consistent results.

The Empire of [[Compendium/Atlas/Material Plane/Rushthan/Droshar/Imperial Heartlands/The Capital/Salren/Salren\|Salren]] has embraced magitech more heavily than any other known nation, using it to power transportation, industry, communications, defenses, and warfare.

---

## Essence Crystals

Nearly all magitech relies on **essence crystals**.

These naturally occurring crystals can store and release magical energy when properly refined.

Essence crystals serve as the fuel source for most magitech devices.

Their quality determines:

- Power output
- Reliability
- Longevity
- Cost

High-grade crystals are valuable strategic resources throughout Droshar.

---

## Common Applications

Magitech appears throughout Imperial society.

Examples include:

- Street lighting
- Industrial machinery
- Elevators and lifts
- Defensive wards
- Transportation systems
- Communications equipment
- Military hardware
- Firearms

Many devices that once required skilled spellcasters can now be operated by trained technicians.

---

## Magitech Firearms

Magitech firearms use essence crystals to accelerate metal projectiles at tremendous speed.

Unlike traditional bows or crossbows, the projectile is launched by a controlled burst of magical energy.

The result is:

- High velocity
- Significant stopping power
- Distinctive noise
- Expensive maintenance

The damage comes from the projectile itself, not from magical energy.

Most users describe the sound as:

> "Thunder without fire."

---

## Limitations

Magitech is powerful, but it is not perfect.

### Crystal Degradation

Essence crystals slowly wear down with use.

Damaged crystals become less efficient and may eventually fail.

### Maintenance

Magitech devices require skilled upkeep and replacement parts.

A neglected machine is often more dangerous than a broken one.

### Cost

Refined essence crystals are expensive.

Many frontier communities cannot afford advanced magitech infrastructure.

### Magical Instability

Regions affected by strong leyline activity, Titan residue, or magical disturbances may interfere with magitech devices.

Failures are uncommon, but they can be dramatic.

---

## Magitech and the Empire

The Empire favors magitech because it is predictable.

A spellcaster must be trained.

A biomancer cannot be controlled.

A machine can be regulated, standardized, and reproduced.

For Imperial authorities, magitech represents order.

It transforms magical power from an individual talent into a managed resource.

---

## Cultural Perception

### Imperial Citizens

Most view magitech as progress.

### Frontier Communities

Many see it as useful but expensive and difficult to maintain.

### Biomancers

Often criticize magitech as artificial and limiting.

### Traditionalists

Sometimes view it as an attempt to replace older practices and knowledge.

### Common Folk

Most simply know that crystals power machines.

The details are left to engineers.

---

## What Most People Believe

Most people do not understand how magitech works.

They know only three things:

- Crystals power it.
- Engineers build it.
- The Empire wants more of it.

Everything beyond that is someone else's job.

> Imperial saying:
>
> “A crystal does not care who turns the key.”
