## Overview
The **House of Veynar** ruled the Empire for over four centuries before being overthrown by [[Compendium/NPC's/Salren/Kael Vorrath\|Emperor Kael Vorrath]]. Their reign was defined by **measured authority, negotiated power, and enduring respect for the frontier and the old covenants of the land**.

They believed the Empire survived not by tightening its grip, but by allowing its many regions to share the burden of balance.

History has not been kind to that belief.

---

## How They Are Remembered
The memory of House Veynar is fractured:

- **In the Imperial heartlands:** remembered as indulgent monarchs who allowed instability to grow unchecked beyond Salren.  
- **In the frontier:** remembered as *the last kings who listened* — to druids, wardens, hunters, and the land itself.  
- **In Salren’s institutions:** quietly blamed for systems that grew too strained to manage without force.  

---

## Symbol & Identity
- **Heraldry:** A **silver stag** upon a **green field**, crowned with a circlet of stars.  
- **Motto:** *“Through Ash and Bone, We Endure.”*  
- **Meaning:**  
  - The stag symbolized vigilance without domination.  
  - The green field represented Droshar’s living world.  
  - The stars recalled ancient oaths made beneath older skies.  

---

## Philosophy of Rule
- **Covenant Over Decree:** Power was shared through treaties, rites, and long memory.  
- **Distributed Balance:** The frontier was trusted to govern its own magic, traditions, and dangers.  
- **Tolerance of the Old Ways:** Druidic circles, Titan-wardens, and biomantic traditions were watched — but not outlawed.  
- **Reluctance to Centralize:** Veynar rulers resisted binding too much power to Salren alone, fearing catastrophic failure if it fell.  

This restraint earned loyalty — and suspicion.

---

## The Fall
- **Accusation:** Kael Vorrath denounced the Veynars as caretakers who mistook patience for wisdom and tolerance for safety.  
- **Coup:** Struck during a Dawn festival, imperial forces seized the throne and the Tablets of Destiny in a single night.  
- **Aftermath:**  
  - Most of the ruling family executed publicly.  
  - Loyalists purged or driven into exile.  
  - Official histories rewritten within a generation.  

The Veynars did not resist openly. Some claim they believed the Empire would tear itself apart if they did.

---

## Legacy
- **In Salren:** Their name is spoken rarely, and never in court.  
- **In the Frontier:** The *Silver Stag* remains a symbol of lawful defiance and remembrance.  
- **Among Dissidents:** A scratched stag mark signals sympathy, not rebellion — yet.  

Children’s tales still speak of rulers who walked the borders themselves and knew the names of rivers.

---

## Notable Figures
- **[[King Alaric Veynar\|King Alaric Veynar]]** — Last crowned monarch, executed during the coup. Remembered as calm, deliberate, and slow to choose violence.  
- **[[Queen Meriel Veynar\|Queen Meriel Veynar]]** — Fate uncertain; some claim she vanished into the western passes before the executions.  
- **[[The Heirs of the Stag\|The Heirs of the Stag]]** — Rumors persist of surviving bloodlines, scattered beyond imperial reach.  

---

## Rumors & Whispers
- **The Silver Crown:** A Veynar relic said to resonate with the Salren convergence — not as a focus, but as a stabilizer.  
- **The Broken Oath:** Some claim the Veynars swore to prevent the world from being bound too tightly — and failed.  
- **The Stag Still Runs:** In Emberreach, hunters whisper that a Veynar heir walks among them, unmarked and unannounced.  
- **The True Betrayal:** It is said the coup required help from within Salren’s own noble houses — names still unspoken.
