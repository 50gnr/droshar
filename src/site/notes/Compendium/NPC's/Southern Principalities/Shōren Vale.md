## Overview

Shōren Vale is the current **Master of Petitions** within the [[Compendium/Atlas/Material Plane/Rushthan/Droshar/Southern Principalities/Southern Principalities\|Southern Principalities]], an administrative office responsible for resolving disputes, trade grievances, shrine conflicts, and matters that fall between the authority of [[Compendium/Atlas/Material Plane/Rushthan/Droshar/Southern Principalities/Southern Principalities\|Southern Principalities]] and [[Compendium/Atlas/Material Plane/Rushthan/Droshar/Southern Principalities/Bardune/Bardune\|Bardune]].

The position grants him access to nearly every pressure point in the South.

Nobles speak to him because he is useful.

Shrine keepers tolerate him because he is respectful.

Merchants fear him because he remembers everything.

Very few know he quietly oversees [[Compendium/Lore/Organizations/The Lotus Choir\|The Lotus Choir]], a network of operatives, informants, negotiators, thieves, and investigators who influence events throughout the Southern Principalities from behind a veil of diplomacy and petitions.

To the public, the Choir is rumor.

To those in power, it is inevitability.

---

## Description

Shōren appears as a refined kitsune man of indeterminate age.

His clothing is immaculate but intentionally forgettable, favoring layered robes in muted colors with subtle lotus and water motifs worked into the fabric.

He rarely raises his voice.

Even in crowded halls, conversations seem to lower naturally around him.

Most people leave meetings with Shōren believing he was reasonable.

Many later discover they agreed to things they never intended.

---

## Public Reputation

Among officials and merchants, Shōren is considered:

- Patient
- Scholarly
- Politically invaluable
- Dangerously informed

Among common folk:

- Some view him as a peacemaker
- Others believe trouble follows wherever his letters arrive

In [[Compendium/Atlas/Material Plane/Rushthan/Droshar/Southern Principalities/Bardune/Bardune\|Bardune]], he is often regarded with suspicion.

> “He trims roots and calls it gardening.”

In [[Compendium/Atlas/Material Plane/Rushthan/Droshar/Southern Principalities/Southern Principalities\|Southern Principalities]], he is viewed more pragmatically.

> “Peace survives because men like him dirty their hands.”

Older officials sometimes describe him as a man who never truly moved on from some long-forgotten tragedy, though few agree on what that tragedy was.

---

## Personality

Shōren rarely speaks directly about ideology.

Instead, he asks questions.

He prefers to guide others toward conclusions rather than argue for them openly.

He almost never displays anger.

Disappointment from him is often more unsettling than threats.

He believes conflict is inevitable.

What matters is controlling its shape.

---

## Philosophy

> “Peace is not natural. Peace is maintained.”

Shōren believes the Southern Principalities are slowly collapsing beneath pride, stagnation, and ritualized inaction.

He sees warning signs everywhere:

- Ancient systems left unattended
- Political leaders afraid to act
- Growing distrust between cities
- Shrines maintained through habit rather than understanding
- Threats ignored until they become crises

To Shōren, catastrophe is rarely sudden.

It arrives after years of hesitation.

Most suffering, in his view, comes not from malice but from delay.

By the time leaders agree action is necessary, the damage is already done.

# Hidden Data

## Role in the Campaign

Shōren initially serves as:

- Patron
- Handler
- Mission coordinator
- Political guide
- Source of intelligence

He provides the party with:

- Assignments
- Operational resources
- Intelligence
- Safehouses
- Context
- Carefully curated truths

The party begins believing they are protecting stability within the Southern Principalities through covert action.

Over time they may discover that Shōren has been deliberately creating crises in order to force change.

Whether he is a villain, patriot, visionary, or fool depends entirely upon perspective.

---



## Relationship to [[Compendium/Lore/Deities/Kaiju/Bakuryō\|Bakuryō]]

Shōren knows the Bakuryō shrines form part of an ancient system meant to guide and appease the river spirit.

He also knows the system is weakening.

Rather than quietly repair it, he chooses to expose its failure.

The offerings recovered by the party are not random valuables.

They are bait.

By removing them, the party helps draw [[Compendium/Lore/Deities/Kaiju/Bakuryō\|Bakuryō]] away from the shrine network.

Once the river spirit is exposed, [[Compendium/NPC's/Southern Principalities/Hoshi Reedmark\|Hoshi Reedmark]] executes the second phase of the operation and flips the hidden scale beneath Bakuryō's jaw.

The spirit's nature reverses.

The rivers reverse.

Hakuryū Valley floods.

To the public, it appears the old system has failed.

To Shōren, that is the point.

---

## Relationship to [[Compendium/NPC's/Southern Principalities/Hoshi Reedmark\|Hoshi Reedmark]]

Hoshi Reedmark is Shōren's most trusted field operative.

Where Shōren plans, Hoshi acts.

She carries out operations too dangerous, too strange, or too morally costly for ordinary agents.

Though fiercely loyal, Hoshi bears far more of the human cost of Shōren's vision than he does.

After the rise of the [[Iron King\|Iron King]], she may become one of the few people capable of honestly acknowledging what Shōren ultimately unleashed.

## True History

Shōren was not born into power.

He was born into a family responsible for maintaining one of the ancient sites connected to the prison of the [[Iron King\|Iron King]].

Their duty was simple:

- Maintain records
- Deliver offerings
- Preserve traditions
- Ensure the prison remained secure

Most caretakers viewed the responsibility as ritual.

Shōren's family understood it was real.

---

## The Childhood Tragedy

When Shōren was young, warning signs began appearing.

- Records disappeared
- Offerings arrived late
- Protective rites were neglected
- Officials argued over responsibility
- Nobody acted

Everyone assumed someone else would solve the problem.

Eventually a containment incident occurred.

Compared to later disasters it was minor.

To a child, it was catastrophic.

His parents died.

Many others died.

Afterward nobody accepted responsibility.

Reports were written.

Committees formed.

Blame was shared.

Nothing changed.

This became the defining event of Shōren's life.

---

## The Lesson He Learned

Shōren did not learn:

> Evil must be stopped.

He learned:

> People do not act until they are forced.

Every major decision in his life grows from that belief.

---

## The Hidden Duty of the Master of Petitions

The modern office is administrative.

The original office was not.

Historically, the Master of Petitions also oversaw the maintenance of several ancient seals, prisons, and forgotten obligations throughout the South.

Most holders of the office ignored those responsibilities.

Shōren embraced them.

Over time he inherited archives and records nobody else bothered to read.

Including records concerning the [[Iron King\|Iron King]].

---

## The Iron King

The records did not describe a monster.

They described a ruler.

A conqueror.

A tyrant.

A unifier.

Over decades Shōren became fascinated by the imprisoned Oni.

Eventually he came to view the Iron King less as a prisoner and more as a source of historical wisdom.

This obsession would become his greatest flaw.

---

## Creation of the Lotus Choir

The Choir was never intended to seize power.

It was built because Shōren no longer trusted governments to respond when necessary.

Its original goals were:

- Gather information
- Bypass bureaucracy
- Solve problems quickly
- Prevent another tragedy like the one that killed his parents

For many years it genuinely improved lives.

The players should be able to find evidence of this.

The Choir was once exactly what Shōren believed it should be.

---

## The Fall of the Vision

As Shōren aged, he saw the same patterns returning:

- Political paralysis
- Growing distrust
- Neglected traditions
- Failing systems
- Leaders afraid to act

He concluded reform would never happen voluntarily.

The South had to be forced into change.

## Arc I — The River Runs Backward

The Bakuryō shrine system is failing.

The flood proves it.

Shōren intentionally uses the party to remove the offerings.

Hoshi flips the scale.

The disaster is deliberate.

---

## Arc II — The Fracture

The theft and staging of a sacred relic deepens distrust between [[Compendium/Atlas/Material Plane/Rushthan/Droshar/Southern Principalities/Southern Principalities\|Southern Principalities]] and [[Compendium/Atlas/Material Plane/Rushthan/Droshar/Southern Principalities/Bardune/Bardune\|Bardune]].

The cities prove they cannot trust one another.

---

## Arc III — The Long Knife

The party unknowingly enables the failed assassination of Aketsu Blackwing.

The leadership crisis proves the ruling class cannot protect itself.

---

## Arc IV — The Last Seal

The Seal of Union is stolen.

Shōren reveals himself as the Quiet Fox.

The South finally unifies.

For a brief moment, it appears he has won.

---

## The Fatal Mistake

Shōren reaches a final conclusion:

> The South needs stronger leadership than its current rulers can provide.

The records of the Iron King offer an answer.

He convinces himself he can use the wisdom of the ancient emperor without repeating the mistakes of the past.

This is the greatest error of his life.

---

## Arc V — The Empty Throne

Shōren releases the [[Iron King\|Iron King]].

Not out of greed.

Not out of madness.

Not out of loyalty.

Out of hope.

He genuinely believes the South is finally ready to be united.

Instead, the Iron King kills him and claims authority for himself.

---

## Core Theme

Shōren is not the villain.

He is the warning.

Nearly every problem he identified was real.

Nearly every fear he held was justified.

His tragedy is that he became convinced that only he could force the world to change.

And in doing so, he created a catastrophe far greater than the one that shaped him.
