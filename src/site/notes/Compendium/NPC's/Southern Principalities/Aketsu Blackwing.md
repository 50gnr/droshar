# Overview

Aketsu Blackwing serves as the current **High Steward of [[Compendium/Atlas/Material Plane/Rushthan/Droshar/Southern Principalities/In'nali/In'nali\|In'nali]]**, the highest administrative and military authority within the mountain city.

To the people of In’nali, Aketsu is not viewed as a ruler in the traditional sense.

He is viewed as:
> the man responsible for ensuring the mountain survives another generation.

In’nali’s isolated position atop the great plateau leaves little room for weakness, inefficiency, or indecision. Every terrace, granary, aqueduct, mine, and shrine must function in harmony for the city to endure.

Aketsu embodies this philosophy completely.

He is disciplined, direct, and famously intolerant of complacency.

---

# Description

Aketsu is a tall tengu with dark iron-black feathers streaked subtly with silver near the wings and temples. Unlike the elaborate robes favored by many southern nobles, his attire is restrained and practical — layered dark ceremonial garments reinforced with leather and scaled plates beneath.

His wings are typically folded tightly behind him unless agitated.

Long scar-lines mark his forearms and collarbone, remnants of old mountain campaigns and ritual vows taken during his ascent through In’nali’s hierarchy.

Aketsu moves with unusual stillness for an avian creature.

When he speaks, rooms tend to quiet instinctively.

---

# Personality

Aketsu values:
- competence
- duty
- discipline
- sacrifice

He has little patience for:
- political games
- emotional indecision
- reckless ambition
- performative honor

Though stern, he is not cruel.

He genuinely believes survival demands structure.

To Aketsu:
hesitation kills cities.

---

# Public Reputation

Within [[Compendium/Atlas/Material Plane/Rushthan/Droshar/Southern Principalities/In'nali/In'nali\|In'nali]], Aketsu is viewed as:
- dependable
- severe
- incorruptible
- deeply loyal to the city

Among soldiers and laborers, he is respected because:
- he routinely walks the terraces himself
- inspects granaries personally
- and has reduced his own household luxuries during lean years

Among the upper houses:
he is sometimes feared.

Aketsu’s loyalty is to In’nali itself — not to noble bloodlines.

In [[Compendium/Atlas/Material Plane/Rushthan/Droshar/Southern Principalities/Bardune/Bardune\|Bardune]], he is often regarded as:
- inflexible
- militaristic
- dangerously pragmatic

---

# Philosophy

> “The mountain does not forgive failure.”

Aketsu believes the Southern Principalities survive only because In’nali remains vigilant.

The city’s isolation taught hard lessons:
- dependence creates weakness
- abundance breeds complacency
- and survival belongs to those prepared to endure hardship

To Aketsu, peace is valuable —
but only when backed by readiness.

---

# Keeper of the Northern Road

Alongside his role as High Steward, Aketsu bears the ceremonial title:
### Keeper of the Northern Road

The Northern Road is:
- In’nali’s only true route in or out
- a sacred and strategic passage
- lined with shrines, ancient wards, and floating stone formations

Protecting the road is considered protecting the city itself.

Aketsu takes this duty personally.

---

# Relationship to [[Compendium/Atlas/Material Plane/Rushthan/Droshar/Southern Principalities/Bardune/Bardune\|Bardune]]

Aketsu respects Bardune’s wisdom and endurance,
but quietly believes the river-city has become:
- too cautious
- too spiritually dependent
- unwilling to act decisively when danger emerges

Where Bardune seeks harmony,
Aketsu seeks stability.

To him:
those are not always the same thing.

---

# Relationship to [[Compendium/NPC's/Southern Principalities/Seiha Riverlily\|Seiha Riverlily]]

Aketsu and [[Compendium/NPC's/Southern Principalities/Seiha Riverlily\|Seiha Riverlily]] maintain a tense but functional relationship.

Neither openly insults the other.
Neither fully agrees with the other’s worldview.

Aketsu views Seiha as:
- wise
- honorable
- dangerously hesitant

Seiha views Aketsu as:
- disciplined
- necessary
- too willing to force outcomes

Despite this, both understand:
the Southern Principalities survive only while the two cities remain united.

---

# Relationship to [[Compendium/Lore/Organizations/The Lotus Choir\|The Lotus Choir]]

Officially:
Aketsu denies the existence of [[Compendium/Lore/Organizations/The Lotus Choir\|The Lotus Choir]] entirely.

Privately:
he knows they exist.

Though he despises covert manipulation within the Principalities, he tolerates certain hidden operations as unavoidable realities of governance.

His greatest concern is not espionage itself.

It is:
> who ultimately benefits from instability.

Aketsu distrusts organizations that answer to individuals rather than institutions.

This places him increasingly at odds with [[Compendium/NPC's/Southern Principalities/Shōren Vale\|Shōren Vale]].

# Role in the Campaign

Aketsu initially appears as:
- distant authority figure
- political obstacle
- stern nationalist
- representative of In’nali’s worldview

Over time, the party may realize:
- many of his warnings about instability were accurate
- he anticipated growing shrine failures
- he suspected interference surrounding [[Bakuryō\|Bakuryō]]
- and he may have already begun preparing In’nali for catastrophe before the floods begin

Whether the players view him as:
- authoritarian
- honorable
- paranoid
- or tragically burdened

…depends entirely on how the South changes.

# DM Notes

Aketsu represents:
> vigilance without manipulation.

Unlike Shōren:
- he acts openly

He is the embodiment of:
- duty
- burden
- preparation

- Direct eye contact

- observing weather during conversation
